Why hashdiff relies on this
01
Deterministic. Same input always gives the same hash — so comparing two loads of the same unchanged record always agrees.
02
Sensitive to tiny changes. Flip one character anywhere in the row and the hash looks completely different — nothing is "almost the same."
03
Fixed size, no matter the input. A satellite row with 3 columns or 30 columns still compresses down to one hash of the same length — cheap to store and compare.
04
One comparison instead of many. This is the whole payoff: instead of checking FILE_DATE, STATUS, and AMOUNT separately, you check one value and trust it reflects all of them.
Anchor phrase: "A hash takes anything, of any length, and turns it into a fixed-size fingerprint — and even a one-character change produces a totally different fingerprint. That's what makes it safe to trust a single hashdiff comparison instead of checking every column."
